About Me

Experience that
complements, not replaces.

My name is Arnold Ruijter. With over 20 years in complex enterprise environments, my goal is to add experience, perspective, and leadership where and when it adds value.

I aim to leverage & embrace existing capability, rather than replacing it. In other words, strengthen what is already there and provide extra focus where it is needed.

"What you see is what you get" — I am open, direct, and transparent. I do not maintain half-truths, and everyone knows where to stand.

I started in IT without highly relevant studies — just a lot of curiosity. From dissecting Excel macros, to Visual Basic, to enterprise-wide architecture. In all those years, I developed my own vision on what I found are the key elements of business IT.

While much of my career has been at enterprise or strategic level, I am equally at home with domain architecture, cross-sector assignments, or engagements at any scale. The level of the role matters less than the quality of the outcome.

My Vision

People, Process & Result

Information Technology is overall quite rational, which is why I prefer the functional part of IT over the purely technical. Functional IT drills down to the very core of company culture:

"We do things a certain way, and this is why. We want to innovate while sticking to those ways as much as possible. If that changes, so should our mindset — which is challenging."

We are open to change because we understand the need for growth. However, with any change activities, the people affected should be heavily involved. It is important that people trust the process, because their role is essential in jointly delivering the desired results.

IT cannot succeed without the involvement of the user community and business SMEs. We should not just tell our stakeholders what is or isn't coming — we should explain the "Why", leading to outcomes that are agreed on, or even jointly decided.

Sure, this cannot always be applied. But at least the "Why" must always be clear.

🧭

Transparency Over Politics

I say what I can and cannot do. I don't maintain "bent truths." That clarity creates trust — and trust creates results.

🤝

Leadership Through Trust

The first time I led people, I tried to copy my manager — and it didn't work. I learned to lead as myself: caring for wellbeing, starting with trust, and relying on people's willingness and capabilities.

🔄

Making Yourself Redundant

The goal of good leadership is to enable your team to set their own actions and make decisions within agreed boundaries. A team that doesn't need you telling them what to do — that's the target.

Hands-on When Needed

Beyond strategic roles, I genuinely enjoy getting closer to the detail. Curiosity and hands-on involvement have always been at the root of how I learn and deliver.
